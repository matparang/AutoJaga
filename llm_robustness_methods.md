| Method | Mechanism | Evidence/Use Case | Limitation |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| **Self-Consistency + Verifier Prompting** | Generates multiple reasoning paths (e.g., chain-of-thought) and selects the answer with highest consistency across paths; adds a verifier step to validate final answers against constraints or facts | Used in GSM8K math benchmark: increased accuracy from 74.4% to 82.1% on LLaMA-2-7B; also improved robustness to adversarial perturbations in reasoning tasks | Computationally expensive (requires multiple model calls per query); performance gains diminish with very complex problems where consistency is hard to achieve |
| **Constitutional AI Fine-tuning** | Trains models using preference pairs where outputs are ranked according to constitutional principles (e.g., "helpful, honest, harmless"); uses rule-based reward modeling instead of human feedback | Anthropic's Claude models show 30-40% reduction in harmful outputs vs. standard RLHF; demonstrated on HH-RLHF dataset with significant improvements in alignment metrics | Requires careful constitution design; may over-constrain creative or nuanced responses; difficult to handle conflicting principles |
| **Process Supervision (Step-by-Step Reward Modeling)** | Trains models to generate intermediate reasoning steps (process supervision) rather than just final answers, with rewards based on correctness of each step | DeepMind's AlphaProof achieved 59% success rate on International Math Olympiad problems vs. 33% for answer-only supervision; improved generalization to unseen problem types | Requires high-quality step-level annotations; more complex training pipeline; potential for reward hacking if step evaluation is imperfect |
