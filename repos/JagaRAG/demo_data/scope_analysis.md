# SCOPE Analysis Summary

## Secondary Compound Profiling for Endangered Species (SCOPE)

### Project Background

SCOPE is a collaborative research initiative focused on documenting the chemical diversity of endangered plant species before they are lost. By understanding the secondary metabolites these species produce, we can:

1. Identify compounds with pharmaceutical potential
2. Develop non-destructive monitoring methods
3. Create chemical "fingerprints" for species identification
4. Guide conservation priorities

### Styrax Benzoin Analysis

#### Sample Processing
- Fresh resin samples stored at -20°C
- Extraction with methanol:water (80:20)
- Analysis by LC-MS/MS and GC-MS

#### Major Compounds Identified

| Compound | Concentration | Activity |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| Cinnamic acid | 12.3% | Anti-inflammatory |
| Benzoic acid | 8.7% | Antimicrobial |
| Vanillin | 4.2% | Antioxidant |
| Styrene | 2.1% | Volatile marker |
| Sumaresinolic acid | 6.8% | Wound healing |

#### Quality Grading

Based on cinnamic acid content:
- **Grade A**: >15% cinnamic acid (Premium quality)
- **Grade B**: 10-15% cinnamic acid (Standard quality)
- **Grade C**: <10% cinnamic acid (Industrial grade)

### Sustainable Harvesting Recommendations

1. **Tapping interval**: Minimum 6 months between harvests
2. **Tree size**: Only tap trees >30cm DBH
3. **Season**: Dry season only (reduced infection risk)
4. **Wound size**: Maximum 10cm × 5cm incision
5. **Recovery period**: No tapping after flowering

### Future Research

- Investigate antimicrobial synergies between compounds
- Develop rapid field test for quality grading
- Map genetic variation across populations
- Test cultivation feasibility for high-yield varieties
