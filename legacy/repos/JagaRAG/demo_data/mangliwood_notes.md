# Mangliwood Specimen Research Notes

## Overview

This document contains field notes from the SCOPE (Secondary Compound Profiling for Endangered species) project studying Mangliwood trees (*Styrax* species) in Southeast Asia.

## Specimen Collection

### Location
- Coordinates: Undisclosed (conservation protection)
- Habitat: Lowland dipterocarp forest
- Elevation: 200-400m

### Specimens Collected
1. **M-001**: Mature tree, bark sample
2. **M-002**: Young tree, leaf samples
3. **M-003**: Resin exudate from wounded bark

## Initial Chemical Analysis

### Benzoin Compounds
The resin samples (M-003) showed high concentrations of:
- Cinnamic acid esters
- Vanillin derivatives
- Sumaresinolic acid

### Antimicrobial Activity
Preliminary assays indicate significant inhibition against:
- *Staphylococcus aureus* (MIC: 64 μg/mL)
- *Escherichia coli* (MIC: 128 μg/mL)
- *Candida albicans* (MIC: 256 μg/mL)

## Conservation Status

Mangliwood trees face threats from:
1. Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion
2. Overharvesting for benzoin resin
3. Climate change affecting flowering cycles

## Research Questions

1. Can sustainable tapping methods be developed that don't harm tree health?
2. What is the minimum viable population for genetic diversity?
3. Are there cultivar varieties that could be plantation-grown?

## Next Steps

- Complete GC-MS analysis of all specimens
- Compare with historical voucher specimens
- Develop harvesting guidelines with local communities
